Integrating Technology with Teaching



Curricular Resources that Guide Instructional Design

You can create a video of yourself pre-recording content or giving the objective for the lessons. This will allow students to know what their goal is and to have an idea of what they should expect to learn. You can also have them take notes on a video that covers the content prior to teaching in the classroom. You can post this on an online forum with key factors or take aways that they must write down. A cool way of doing this is having them take "Cornell Notes" which has them draw out what particular concepts are interesting and has them use a variety of colors within their notes.

Formative Assessment Strategy

Within the classroom setting you can use technology to check for understanding throughout lessons or chunks. You can do this by playing games within the classroom or at home having them complete exit tickets on specific lessons. For example: I could have students play a review game such as kahoots in class to cover potential vs kinetic energy. When students are done with the game it automatically scores for them and as a teacher you can go into the review mode and see what students missed what questions. I can use this information to provide extra support within the classroom through re-teaching material so students can gain more confidence.

Summative Assessment Strategy

Tech can also be used to assist with summative assessments. You can have students write a paper using Microsoft word or google docs if you are teaching RLA. Another method is simply putting the unit exam on the computer for them to complete. This is helpful because you can set the computer to grade the exams for you which helps teachers to quickly input grades. For my example summative assessment I used quizlet to create a quiz. My quiz is to ensure mastery of TEKS 6.8A “Compare and contrast potential and kinetic energy” which is a 6th grade science TEK. An appropriate platform for this summative assessment is blackboard which is an online classroom that my district uses. Once students complete the assessment, I will be able to automatic scores for each student. This will allow me to know if there are particular students that need additional support or if it's a concept that requires me to re-teach.

Using Student Data

Most school districts are using online technology to communicate and store grades for their students. Having things like formative and summative assessments being conducted using technology allows for teachers to quickly grade the work and then synthesize this information to help form their plan moving forward.
